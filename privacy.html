Privacy Policy — DJ Transition Finder

Last updated: 15 June 2026

DJ Transition Finder is a browser extension that identifies the track you're playing on SoundCloud and gives you links to related DJ sets, charts, and store pages. We take a minimal-data approach.

What we collect

Nothing is collected, transmitted, or sold. The extension does not send your browsing activity, identity, or usage to us or any third party.

Data stored on your device

Your lookup history and favorites are saved locally in your browser (via the browser's extension storage) so the extension can show them back to you. This data never leaves your device, and you can clear it at any time from within the extension or by removing the extension.

Network activity

The extension reads the track name from the SoundCloud page you're viewing. When you click a link (e.g. to 1001tracklists, Beatport, YouTube, Spotify, or a Google search), that opens the respective third-party website in a new tab — those sites' own privacy policies then apply.

Advanced users may optionally configure their own self-hosted backend URL in the extension's options. If you do, track lookups are sent only to the server you control. By default, no such backend is configured and no lookups are sent anywhere.

Third-party data

Song key and BPM data, where shown, is provided by GetSongBPM.
